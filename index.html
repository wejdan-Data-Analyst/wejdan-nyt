The Saudi Analyst Powering the Future of Urban Intelligence

How Wejdan Awaji transformed traffic systems across five major cities, reducing accidents by 38% through AI-powered analytics
By Sarah Chen | Technology Correspondent
$50M
Smart Mobility project revolutionizing Saudi urban infrastructure

In the bustling streets of Riyadh, where traditional meets futuristic, a quiet revolution is taking place. At the helm of this transformation is Wejdan Mohammed Awaji, a 29-year-old data strategist whose innovative approach to urban intelligence is reshaping how entire cities think, respond, and evolve.

As Accenture's Data Strategy Lead, Awaji has spearheaded a groundbreaking $50 million Smart Mobility initiative that integrates real-time traffic data with AI-powered response systems across five major Saudi cities. The results speak volumes: a 38% reduction in traffic accidents and a complete reimagining of urban infrastructure management.

We're not just analyzing data – we're creating a nervous system for our cities, one that can predict, adapt, and protect its citizens in real-time.

Awaji's journey began at the University of Jeddah, where she earned her Bachelor's degree in Data Science and Analysis with an impressive 4.21/5.00 GPA. But it was her work at the SDAIA T5 Bootcamp that first showcased her unique ability to bridge complex machine learning algorithms with practical urban solutions.

Her innovative license plate recognition system, originally developed during her bootcamp experience, evolved into a comprehensive traffic intelligence network. Using advanced neural networks and CNN models, the system doesn't just detect violations – it predicts traffic patterns, identifies potential accident hotspots, and automatically dispatches emergency services when needed.

What sets Awaji apart is her holistic approach to urban intelligence. Her background spans from brain tumor prediction using EfficientNetB0 models to NLP-powered sentiment analysis, giving her a unique perspective on how AI can serve human needs across multiple domains.

"The future of smart cities isn't just about technology," Awaji explains from her office overlooking Riyadh's transformed skyline. "It's about creating systems that understand human behavior, predict community needs, and respond with both efficiency and empathy."

As urban populations continue to grow globally, Awaji's work in Saudi Arabia serves as a blueprint for intelligent city management. Her integration of Azure cloud architecture with advanced machine learning models demonstrates how emerging economies can leapfrog traditional infrastructure limitations.

With future projects focusing on predictive healthcare systems and sustainable energy management, Wejdan Awaji represents a new generation of data scientists who see technology not as an end goal, but as a means to create more livable, responsive, and intelligent urban environments for all.
